Data & Statistics

Grouped frequency tables, estimated mean, scatter graphs, two-way tables and combined probability

Grouped frequency tables and estimated mean

When data is grouped into classes (e.g. 0–9, 10–19…), you cannot find the exact mean. Instead, use the midpoint of each class as a representative value.

Estimated mean = Σ(midpoint × frequency) ÷ Σ(frequency)
Midpoint = (lower bound + upper bound) ÷ 2
💡 Modal class: The group with the highest frequency is called the modal class — not a single value like the mode.

Scatter graphs and correlation

A scatter graph plots two variables against each other to look for a relationship. The strength and direction of the relationship is called correlation.

Positive
Both increase together
Negative
One increases, other decreases
None
No pattern / scattered
Line of best fit
Drawn through the middle
💡 Line of best fit: Should pass through approximately the mean point (x̄, ȳ) with roughly equal numbers of points on each side.

Two-way tables

A two-way table organises data by two categories simultaneously, showing the frequency for each combination. Row totals and column totals must all add up to the grand total.

💡 Missing values: Use totals to find missing cells. If row total = 50 and one cell = 32, the other cell = 50 − 32 = 18.

Combined probability

When two events happen independently, you can find the probability of both occurring (AND) or either occurring (OR).

P(A AND B) = P(A) × P(B)  ← independent events
P(A OR B) = P(A) + P(B) − P(A AND B)
For mutually exclusive events: P(A OR B) = P(A) + P(B)
AND
Multiply probabilities
OR
Add (then subtract overlap)
Independent
One event doesn't affect other
💡 Tree diagrams are useful for combined probability — they help you track AND (multiply along branches) and OR (add the final outcomes you want).

4Combined probability (AND)
▼
A coin is flipped and a dice is rolled. What is the probability of getting heads AND a 6?
  1. 1P(heads) = 1/2
  2. 2P(6) = 1/6
  3. 3Independent events: P(heads AND 6) = 1/2 × 1/6 = 1/12
✅ P(heads AND 6) = 1/12 ≈ 0.083
5Combined probability — tree diagram
▼
A bag has 3 red and 2 blue counters. One is drawn and replaced. Then another is drawn. Find P(both same colour).
  1. 1P(red) = 3/5, P(blue) = 2/5 (with replacement — same each time)
  2. 2P(RR) = 3/5 × 3/5 = 9/25
  3. 3P(BB) = 2/5 × 2/5 = 4/25
  4. 4P(same) = 9/25 + 4/25 = 13/25 = 0.52
✅ P(both same colour) = 13/25 = 0.52
6Describing correlation
▼
A scatter graph plots temperature (x-axis) against ice cream sales (y-axis). The points cluster along a rising line from bottom-left to top-right. Describe the correlation and use the line of best fit to estimate sales at 28°C if the line passes through (20, 150) and (30, 250).
  1. 1Rising from left to right = positive correlation. As temperature increases, ice cream sales increase.
  2. 2Gradient: (250−150)÷(30−20) = 100÷10 = 10 sales per °C
  3. 3At 28°C: 150 + (10 × 8) = 150 + 80 = 230 sales
✅ Strong positive correlation  |  Estimated sales at 28°C = 230
